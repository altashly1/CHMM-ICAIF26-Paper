\subsection{Stylized facts, state count, and the spectral diagnostic}
\label{sec:res_stylized}

All three Cont stylized facts hold on both SPY windows (Figure~\ref{fig:stylized_facts}; Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}, Observed row): the marginal is heavy-tailed (in-sample excess kurtosis $7.68$, out-of-sample $5.29$), linear autocorrelation in the raw growth rate is negligible, and volatility clustering in $|G_t|$ is persistent. A stationary block bootstrap at mean block length $20$ gives broadly overlapping IS and OoS $95\%$ CIs of $[2.17, 12.40]$ and $[0.90, 8.26]$ on the excess-kurtosis point estimates, so kurtosis targets should be read against the joint envelope rather than the points. Sweeping $K \in \{3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21\}$ for CHMM-N on the IS window, the $K = 3$ versus $K = 6$ comparison could not be separated under four-fold and six-fold rolling-origin cross-validation (mean held-out log-likelihood differences of $0.007$ and $0.027$ nats per observation, flipping sign between fold designs); with only four or six fold differences we treat these comparisons as descriptive. BIC and CAIC selected $K = 3$, and we chose $K^\star = 3$ as a parsimony choice, while $K = 18$ had a substantially lower mean held-out log-likelihood at both cadences.

Absolute growth-rate ACF-MAE was nearly flat across the whole sweep, consistent with equation~\eqref{eq:acf_normalised}: a direct effective-rank diagnostic showed a single non-unit eigenvalue carrying $93.6\%$ of the total absolute lag-1 modal contribution (as defined in Section~\ref{sec:spectral}) at $K = 18$ on SPY, and $96.8\%$ at $K^\star = 3$, so the $K - 1$ rank bound did not bind at $K \ge 3$ on this instance; the underlying eigenvector matrices are well conditioned (condition numbers $1.4$ at $K^\star = 3$ and $7.3$ at $K = 18$), so the modal weights are numerically stable. Repeating the diagnostic on the 30-ticker cross-section at the same evaluated $K = 18$, under the same modulus definition (the SPY control reproduces its $0.936$), gave a materially wider distribution: median dominant-mode share $0.726$ (IQR $[0.583, 0.809]$), minimum $0.287$ (NEM), and a median of $7$ non-unit eigen-contributions (maximum $13$; members of complex-conjugate pairs counted separately, so a real damped-oscillatory mode contributes two) needed to reach $95\%$ of the total absolute lag-1 contribution. The effective rank therefore stays well below the $K - 1 = 17$ algebraic maximum across the panel, but the near-one-mode behaviour is SPY-specific: the typical ticker spreads the lag-1 contribution over several eigen-contributions, and whether marginal flexibility dominates additional ACF modes on those tickers is not established here.

\subsection{Generator comparison}
\label{sec:res_generators}

With the state count fixed, we fit the four CHMM variants on the SPY IS window at $K^\star = 3$ and score them against the benchmark generators (Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison}). CHMM-N lands at $91.5\%$ IS and $78.0\%$ OoS KS, simulated excess kurtosis $3.83$ and $3.62$, and $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE $0.0462$ (on par with GARCH at $0.0490$; ACF-MAE is scored on the IS window, and the clustering edge is window-specific: on the stored OoS values CHMM-N, at $0.0544$, sits at the bootstrap floor of $0.0542$ and behind the heavy-tailed CHMM rows at $0.0498$ to $0.0502$, though still ahead of GARCH at $0.0593$). The shared-$\nu$ Student-$t$ provides the preferred marginal-fit and heavy-tail trade-off, the best CHMM OoS KS together with a smaller kurtosis gap than CHMM-N and no penalty hyperparameter, at $91.9\%$ IS and $82.1\%$ OoS KS with simulated excess kurtosis $4.68$ and $4.46$ against observed $7.68$ and $5.29$; on the kurtosis distance alone the penalty-free CHMM-L and CHMM-GED rows sit closer to observed, at lower KS. (A penalised per-state CHMM-t at $\lambda = 20$, with $\lambda$ tuned at $K = 18$ and re-used at $K^\star = 3$, overshot to IS excess kurtosis $18.87$ through a $1/\nu_k$-shrinkage artefact and is treated as a sensitivity reference only.) The four CHMM variants have numerically similar mean OoS sample-CRPS (CHMM-N $1.0393$, CHMM-GED $1.0398$, shared-$\nu$ CHMM-t $1.0406$, CHMM-L $1.0432$, against $1.0398$ for the bootstrap and $1.0440$ for GARCH(1,1)): CRPS does not distinguish the rows at this precision, and the preferred shared-$\nu$ row is selected on the joint KS--kurtosis--regularisation trade-off rather than on kurtosis alone. On volatility clustering the fitted CHMM reduces the lag-252 $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE from $0.0628$ at the i.i.d.\ floor to $0.0462$ on the SPY 2014--2024 instance---capturing substantial slow-decay persistence, though not the panel's best ACF fit (the GARCH-type rows reach $0.0284$ to $0.0490$)---where the Ryd\'{e}n et al.~\cite{ryden1998stylized} $K = 2$ to $3$ Gaussian setup judged the ACF fit unsatisfactory; the raw growth-rate ACF-MAE closes the negligible-linear-autocorrelation fact at $0.0235$ to $0.0240$, indistinguishable from the i.i.d.\ baselines. MS-GARCH stays well below the CHMM on KS under both point-estimate and Bayesian posterior-predictive variants. The reduced QuantGAN negative control fails the heavy-tailed-marginal and volatility-clustering diagnostics at this sample size ($0.0\%$ KS, excess kurtosis $0.56$, and $|G_t|$ ACF-MAE at the i.i.d.\ floor), while its raw-return ACF remains close to the negligible-autocorrelation baseline.

\subsection{Tail index}
\label{sec:res_tail}

Because excess kurtosis is a fragile fourth-moment functional under heavy tails, we complement it with a threshold-local Hill estimate~\cite{hill1975tail} applied identically to the observed series and every simulated family at $K^\star = 3$ (top $5\%$ of $|G_t|$ exceedances); for the light-tailed mixture families this quantity is a shape diagnostic of the estimated threshold range, not an asymptotic tail index. The observed tail index is $\hat\alpha = 3.15$ IS and $3.30$ OoS, inside the $(2, 5)$ band reported by Cont~\cite{cont2001empirical} for daily equity returns, with a block-bootstrap $95\%$ CI of $[2.45, 4.25]$ on the IS estimate. The shared-$\nu$ CHMM-t lands at $\hat\alpha = 3.67$ on both windows with an across-path $95\%$ band of $[3.11, 4.38]$ overlapping the observed CI. With only about $28$ exceedances in the OoS top-$5\%$, we read this as agreement within the finite-threshold uncertainty ranges---the point estimates sit on the thin side---and draw no conclusion about far-tail or asymptotic behaviour; interval overlap is not an equality test. Every headline emission family produces an in-band estimate at $K^\star = 3$ (CHMM-N $3.41$, CHMM-L $3.26$, CHMM-GED $3.24$) despite IS excess kurtosis ranging from $3.83$ to $5.45$ across those rows, and the penalised sensitivity row remains in band while reaching kurtosis $18.87$: at this threshold and sample size, the Hill statistic does not discriminate reliably among emission families. Applied per tail side, the estimator also recovers the sign of the observed gain/loss asymmetry from the regime means alone.

\subsection{Cross-ticker generalisation and refit}
\label{sec:res_crossticker}

The cross-ticker panel uses the penalised per-state CHMM-t at $K^\star = 3$ and $\lambda = 20$---the specification treated as a sensitivity reference in the SPY comparison---fit one model per ticker on the sector-balanced 30-ticker panel; the panel claims in this subsection therefore attach to that configuration, not to the shared-$\nu$ or Gaussian headline rows. The in-sample KS distribution is concentrated (median $96.8\%$) and the out-of-sample distribution is wider, with median $69.1\%$, mean $66.2 \pm 28.2\%$, and $11/30$ tickers below $60\%$. On the kurtosis axis the same configuration is materially weaker than on SPY: the median simulated-minus-observed excess-kurtosis residual is $+7.18$ (IQR $[0.34, 21.19]$), with several tickers overshooting to simulated excess kurtosis above $60$ through the $1/\nu_k$-shrinkage artefact noted in Section~\ref{sec:res_generators}, so the panel evidence supports the marginal-KS axis but not the kurtosis axis for this configuration. The deepest KS failures are tickers that introduced a new regime out of sample: LLY and UNH in Health Care, NEM in Materials. An ANOVA on OoS KS by sector---run on the $K = 18$ fits of the same tickers rather than this $K^\star = 3$ configuration---returned no significant sector effect on either the original $n = 3$ design or an $n = 6$ expansion to a 60-ticker panel, so the visible concentration of failures on regime-introducing tickers is a per-ticker observation rather than evidence of sector-level structure. Periodic refit closes much of the KS gap: refitting the same specification every $63$ trading days on a rolling $1{,}260$-day window shifts the OoS KS median from $69.1\%$ to $84.7\%$ and reduces failures from $11/30$ to $8/30$.

\subsection{Filter-conditional Value-at-Risk}
\label{sec:res_var}

We back-test the filter-conditional VaR of equation~\eqref{eq:filter_var} on CHMM-N, the Gaussian variant: the forward filter runs under IS-fixed parameters $(\mathbf T, \{\boldsymbol\theta_k\})$, only the one-step-ahead state-probability vector updates through the OoS window, and the VaR is the $\alpha$-quantile of the predictive mixture. The back-test covers all $573$ held-out forecasts (Table~\ref{tab:cond_var}). At VaR level $\alpha = 0.05$ the CHMM rows are not rejected by Christoffersen-cc ($p_{\text{cc}} \in [0.39, 0.67]$ across CHMM configurations), and CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ is likewise not rejected by the DQ test ($p = 0.09$); non-rejection at these sample sizes indicates compatibility with correct conditional coverage rather than a demonstration of it. Breach rates at $\alpha = 0.05$ sit close to nominal, from $26$ breaches ($4.54\%$) at $K = 18$ to $36$ ($6.28\%$) at $K^\star = 3$, and no CHMM row is rejected by Kupiec or Christoffersen-ind at that level. The $\alpha = 0.01$ rows are also not rejected by Christoffersen-cc ($p_{\text{cc}} \in [0.14, 0.16]$) but are power-bounded at $\TOoS{573}$, where only about $5.7$ breaches are expected; the richer DQ specification separates the state counts, rejecting CHMM-N at $K = 18$ ($p = 0.02$) while $K^\star = 3$ escapes rejection only marginally ($p = 0.06$). Against the two non-state-space contenders the CHMM rows reach approximate parity at $\alpha = 0.05$; at $\alpha = 0.01$ only CHMM-N at $K^\star = 3$ is not rejected by DQ, a power-bounded non-rejection rather than evidence of a significant difference, since failure to reject does not establish the null and no pairwise model-comparison test was conducted. The filtered bootstrap ($p = 0.04$) and CAViaR ($p = 0.01$) are both rejected at that tier, as is every other CHMM family and state count in the full sixteen-row family panel (not shown; $p$ from $0.0005$ to $0.030$). Extending to six rolling-origin walk-forward folds, Christoffersen-cc does not reject on $19$ of $24$ rows at the $5\%$ test level, with the five rejections concentrated on stress folds: all four W2 (COVID 2020) rows reject, while W4 (2022 rate-hike onset) produces a single tail-tier rejection at $K = 18$, $\alpha = 0.01$; we read these multi-row panels as multiplicity-aware descriptive evidence rather than repeated confirmations.

\subsection{Configuration map and validation summary}
\label{sec:res_validation}

Because the preceding subsections intentionally exercise different members of the CHMM family, Table~\ref{tab:validation} names the exact configuration behind each headline claim and collects the walk-forward, cross-ticker, state-selection, and spectral evidence referenced above. No single configuration wins every axis: CHMM-N carries the risk head and the walk-forward evidence, the shared-$\nu$ CHMM-t the SPY marginal and tail trade-off, and the penalised CHMM-t the cross-ticker panel. We accordingly read the results as evidence about the CHMM family under a common estimation and evaluation harness, with the shared-$\nu$ specification the recommended default for new single-ticker fits and CHMM-N the reference configuration for the filter-conditional risk head.
