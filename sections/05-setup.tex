The single-asset analysis uses daily SPY (SPDR S\&P~500 ETF) VWAP prices from January~3, 2014 to January~3, 2024, a ten-year in-sample (IS) window of $\TIS{2{,}516}$ trading days; January~4, 2024 through April~20, 2026 is held out for out-of-sample (OoS) evaluation, giving $\TOoS{572}$ growth rates for the generator diagnostics. The VaR back-tests additionally prepend the boundary growth rate into the first held-out session, so their forecast count is $573$. Prices are the vendors' stored daily volume-weighted average price field: Polygon.io consolidated aggregates through December 31, 2024, and Alpaca Markets IEX-feed bars (a single-exchange feed carrying a small fraction of consolidated volume) from January 3, 2025. The feeds do not overlap and the switch falls inside the OoS window, so it remains an unresolved OoS confound. Descriptively, an unadjusted pre/post two-sample KS does not reject equality for VWAP returns ($D = 0.099$, $p = 0.13$) or close returns ($D = 0.065$, $p = 0.59$), but the test is time-confounded and not dependence-calibrated, the segments differ in volatility and kurtosis, and VaR breaches splitting proportionally across them ($17/250$ versus $19/323$ at $\alpha = 0.05$) is a descriptive check rather than evidence of feed equivalence. Series are split-adjusted but not dividend-adjusted, so growth rates are price returns---the object of the price-path stylized facts studied here---at the cost of ex-dividend price drops entering the marginal tails. Cross-ticker generalisation uses a sector-balanced 30-ticker panel spanning ten GICS sectors with three large-cap representatives each, fit one CHMM per ticker.

The CHMM is benchmarked against one representative per model class, all fit on the same IS window: a plain i.i.d.\ empirical bootstrap~\cite{efron1979bootstrap} as the strongest non-parametric baseline; GARCH(1,1) with Gaussian~\cite{bollerslev1986generalized} and Student-$t$~\cite{bollerslev1987conditionally} innovations; MS-GARCH~\cite{haas2004new} as the closest regime-switching comparator, with a Bayesian re-run via the \texttt{MSGARCH} package~\cite{ardia2019msgarch}; an explicit-duration HSMM-N reference at $K = 3$ with Gaussian emissions and a truncated discrete-Pareto sojourn law $p(d;\alpha) \propto d^{-(\alpha+1)}$ on $d \in \{1, \ldots, 200\}$, whose per-state shape update uses the untruncated-Pareto expression $\hat\alpha = 1/\overline{\log(d/d_{\min})}$ and thus ignores the $\alpha$-dependent truncated normaliser, so the duration block is moment-approximate and the row is labelled moment-updated rather than maximum-likelihood; and an in-house QuantGAN~\cite{wiese2020quantgan}, a materially smaller approximation of the published architecture, as the deep-generative negative control. For every model we simulate $1{,}000$ independent paths matching the scored window~\cite{stenger2024thinking}, under deterministic seeds.

We score three primary metrics. The two-sample Kolmogorov--Smirnov (KS) pass rate at $\alpha = 0.05$ counts the fraction of simulated paths whose marginal is not rejected against the observed window; because the asymptotic KS null assumes i.i.d.\ observations while both series carry serial dependence, we read this pass rate as a descriptive marginal-fidelity score rather than a calibrated test. Mean simulated excess kurtosis is read against the observed value on the same window. The mean absolute error between observed and simulated ACFs of absolute growth rates over $L = 252$ lags, computed per path and averaged,
\begin{equation}
    \text{ACF-MAE} = \frac{1}{P}\sum_{p=1}^{P}\frac{1}{L}\sum_{\tau=1}^{L} \left| \hat\rho^{\text{obs}}_{|G|}(\tau) - \hat\rho^{\,\text{sim},(p)}_{|G|}(\tau) \right|,
    \label{eq:acf_mae}
\end{equation}
captures volatility clustering, the third Cont stylized fact~\cite{cont2001empirical}. The VaR panel reports the unconditional Kupiec coverage test~\cite{kupiec1995techniques}, the Christoffersen joint conditional-coverage test~\cite{christoffersen1998evaluating} as the primary backtest, and the Engle--Manganelli dynamic-quantile (DQ) test~\cite{engle2004caviar}, against filtered-bootstrap~\cite{barone1999var} and CAViaR~\cite{engle2004caviar} contenders on the same harness. Because the asymptotic KS null is not calibrated under dependence, we corroborate the KS ordering with a continuous distance: on OoS Wasserstein-1 the stored panel orders bootstrap $0.193$, shared-$\nu$ CHMM-t $0.236$, CHMM-GED $0.252$, CHMM-L $0.264$, CHMM-N $0.286$, GARCH(1,1) $0.355$, MS-GARCH $0.368$, broadly preserving the KS ranking (one swap: CHMM-L moves ahead of CHMM-N), with Anderson--Darling and Hellinger panels behaving similarly; the continuous ranked probability score (CRPS)~\cite{gneiting2007strictly} is reported as a tie-break.
